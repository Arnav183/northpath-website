What clients receive

Every engagement ends with artifacts a client team can run with, not a slide deck. The four below are the core set, shown as mock samples of what each could look like. Real deliverables are larger, specific to the project, and covered by NDA.

A rubric raters apply the same way

Every evaluation engagement ships a rubric document: weighted criteria, pass and fail anchors, and worked examples pulled from adjudicated cases. Two raters applying it independently should land on the same score, and we measure whether they do.

The document itself is the deliverable. Criteria are versioned as guidelines evolve, and every ambiguous case that reaches adjudication becomes a worked example in the next revision.

Preference data with verified justifications

Each pairwise comparison records a direction, a strength, and a written justification tied to specific lines in the responses. Claims are verified before the judgment ships: math recomputed, code rerun, citations checked against the source.

Justifications name the decisive difference, not a vague preference. A judgment that cannot say why it preferred one response gets sent back, not shipped.

Eval reports built on a failure taxonomy

Reports do not stop at scores. Findings are grouped into a failure taxonomy with reproducible examples, so an engineering team knows what to fix first and a data team knows what to collect next.

Every failure in the report links back to the tasks that produced it. If a finding cannot be reproduced from the shipped records, it does not go in the report.

Structured records your pipeline can consume

A judgment is only useful if a training or analysis pipeline can ingest it. So every judgment also ships as a machine-readable record like the sample on the right: one task, one verdict, and the checks that were run before it shipped. Reading it top to bottom:

preference, strength
which response won, and how decisively
failures_a
what went wrong in the losing response, tagged from the failure taxonomy
verification
the checks a human ran before this record was allowed to ship

This sample is the record produced by the interactive sample task. An engagement produces hundreds, in whatever schema the client pipeline expects.
