services

What we do

Model evaluation and red-teaming
We review model outputs against explicit criteria and design rubrics that independent raters apply consistently, with inter-rater agreement measured to prove it. Red-team engagements target specific risks: unsafe completions, jailbreaks, prompt injection through tool results and retrieved context, and gaps between the intended policy and the model's actual behavior. The failure modes we document most: sycophancy, fabricated citations, arithmetic slips, false refusals, and rubric gaming.
Training data and human feedback
Human feedback data for post-training is our core production work: preference rankings, rubric-scored ratings, and written critiques of model responses. We also audit existing datasets for label noise and inconsistent guidelines, then fix the guidelines rather than the symptoms. Ambiguous items go to adjudication, and resolved cases become worked examples in the next revision.
AI adoption advisory
For teams adopting AI, we scope where a model actually fits the workflow and where it will fail quietly. The output is specific: which tasks to hand to a model, what to measure before launch, and what to watch after.
Custom AI tooling
Small, focused tools are part of most engagements: eval harnesses, annotation interfaces, and scripts that turn raw outputs into reviewable data. Everything ships as plain code your team can read, run, and change.

work

Selected engagements

Anonymized summaries of recent engagements. Client names and project specifics are withheld under NDA.

Preference data for reward-model training
Domain experts compared model responses pairwise across mathematics, statistics, and code. Each comparison shipped with a preference direction and strength, a written justification, and independent verification of every claim: math recomputed, code rerun.
Eval design for document-grounded assistants
The client needed evaluation tasks for assistants that answer questions over realistic document sets: statements, forms, and spreadsheets. Each task shipped with a weighted rubric, graded reference responses, and documented failure modes.
Stress-test tasks for research agents
We wrote research questions that current models answer confidently and wrong, each requiring synthesis across multiple independent sources. Every task shipped with a verified answer and the evidence trail behind it.
Review and quality control of annotation work
Reviewers checked other annotators' preference rankings and failure-mode reports against the underlying artifacts before approval. Verified corrections went back into the guidelines so the same mistakes stopped appearing.
Communication-quality grading for assistant models
Graders scored candidate responses on tone, voice, and formatting against a shared anchor scale. Calibration logs and cited rationales kept scoring consistent across the team.

All of this work runs on tooling we build ourselves: capture and verification utilities, scoring panels, and local dashboards, shipped as plain code. If you have a project like these, send a short note.

about

About

Northpath Consulting is an AI development and consulting firm in the Atlanta, Georgia area. The team does the work itself: reviewing model outputs, writing evals, producing feedback data, and building tooling.

Our client work includes frontier AI labs, AI data vendors, and engineering teams putting models into production. Deliverables arrive in the format the pipeline needs: JSONL datasets, rubric documents with anchors and worked examples, and eval reports with a failure taxonomy.

Engagements are scoped and direct: a defined deliverable, a fixed window, and a senior consultant accountable for the result. Most projects run a few weeks inside the client's existing tools and channels.
